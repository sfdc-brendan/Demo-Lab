# DSE Engagement Patterns

Frameworks for the three primary DSE engagement modes: must-win deal consultancy, territory/OU planning, and product partnership.

---

## Must-Win Deal Engagement

### When You're Deployed

DSEs are called in as "special forces" when:
- CRO or RVP has flagged the deal as a Big Bet or must-win
- The architecture is complex (multi-cloud, multi-system, or highly regulated)
- The competitive pressure requires executive-level credibility
- The SE team needs coaching on how to present at C-suite altitude
- A prior attempt stalled and fresh strategic thinking is needed

### Engagement Framework

#### Phase 1: Rapid Account Download (Day 0–1)
Gather from the account team:
- Deal stage, timeline, and decision criteria
- Stakeholder map: economic buyer, technical evaluator, champion, blocker
- Competitive landscape: who else is in the deal, what's their pitch
- Prior demos or proposals: what resonated, what didn't
- Technical environment: existing systems, integration points, constraints
- Political dynamics: internal politics, org changes, M&A context

#### Phase 2: Win Theme Development (Day 1–2)
Identify 2–3 win themes where Salesforce has clear differentiation:
- Map each theme to a specific stakeholder's priority
- Validate themes against competitive positioning
- Ensure at least one theme is architectural (platform-level, not feature-level)

**Win theme structure:**
```
Theme: [Name]
Stakeholder: [Who cares most]
Their priority: [In their words]
Our differentiation: [Why we win here]
Proof point: [Demo, reference, or architecture]
```

#### Phase 3: Demo & Proof Strategy (Day 2–5)
Design the technical win approach:
- Executive demo narrative (situation-complication-resolution arc)
- Technical deep-dive for evaluators (architecture walkthrough)
- Proof of concept scope if needed (minimal viable proof, not boil-the-ocean)
- Objection preparation matrix

#### Phase 4: Executive Engagement (Day of)
Lead or support the presentation:
- Open by demonstrating understanding of their business
- Present insights, not features
- Demo as a narrative, not a feature tour
- Close with clear mutual next steps

#### Phase 5: Debrief & Handoff (Day +1)
- What resonated (and what didn't)
- Updated competitive intelligence
- Technical follow-up items
- Coaching notes for the account SE

## Territory / OU Planning

### Planning Framework

#### Step 1: Landscape Analysis
Map the territory across three dimensions:

| Dimension | Data Points |
|---|---|
| **Segment** | Enterprise, Commercial, Growth — deal size and cycle patterns |
| **Industry** | Vertical concentration, regulatory environment, digital maturity |
| **Product whitespace** | What's installed vs. what's possible — expansion opportunity |

#### Step 2: Account Tiering
Prioritize accounts into three tiers:

| Tier | Criteria | DSE Involvement |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** (3–5 accounts) | Highest strategic value, complex architecture, exec relationships | Direct engagement, co-own strategy |
| **Tier 2** (5–10 accounts) | Strong expansion potential, moderate complexity | On-call consultancy, asset creation |
| **Tier 3** (remaining) | Standard opportunities | Enable through reusable plays and assets |

#### Step 3: GTM Play Mapping
For each segment/industry intersection:
- Identify the primary pain point and trigger event
- Design or select a GTM play
- Map reusable assets (demos, POVs, case studies)
- Assign SE coverage model

#### Step 4: Resource Allocation Model
```
SE allocation framework:
- Tier 1 accounts: named SE + DSE strategic overlay
- Tier 2 accounts: named SE + DSE on-call
- Tier 3 accounts: pooled SE + GTM plays + self-service assets
```

#### Step 5: Quarterly Review Cadence
| Milestone | Frequency | Participants | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pipeline review | Weekly | SE + AE | Deal progression, blocker removal |
| Territory review | Monthly | SE leads + DSE + RVP | GTM play effectiveness, resource adjustment |
| Strategic review | Quarterly | DSE + VP SE + RVP + AD | Territory strategy, account tiering updates |

## Product Partnership

### Voice of the Customer Process

#### Signal Collection
Maintain a running log of field signals:
- Customer requests during demos and evaluations
- Workarounds SEs are building repeatedly
- Competitive losses attributed to capability gaps
- Feature requests from Big Bet or strategic accounts

#### Signal Aggregation
Group signals into themes, not individual requests:

```
Theme: [Name]
Signal count: [X deals / Y customers]
Revenue at risk: [$Z pipeline]
Segments affected: [List]
Current workaround: [What the field does today]
Competitive gap: [Who has this and we don't]
```

#### PM Engagement
Present themes to PM with:
1. Quantified business impact (pipeline, win rate, segment)
2. Customer verbatims (anonymized if needed)
3. Proposed direction (not a PRD — a strategic suggestion)
4. Priority rationale relative to other field asks

#### Feedback Loop
After PM reviews:
- Communicate roadmap decisions back to the field
- Update GTM plays with workarounds or timeline expectations
- Flag any deals that need immediate attention due to gaps

## Executive Engagement Principles

### Credibility Builders
- Reference their public filings, strategy documents, or industry data
- Speak in their business language, not Salesforce product names
- Acknowledge complexity honestly — don't oversimplify
- Share relevant cross-industry patterns ("We've seen similar challenges in...")

### Credibility Destroyers
- Reading from slides
- Feature-dumping without business context
- Claiming capabilities that don't exist
- Being unable to answer "why should I care?"
- Ignoring competitive context when asked

### The 60-Second Test
Every DSE-level engagement should pass this test:

> If a C-suite executive asked "Why should I spend 60 seconds on this?" — could you answer with a clear, compelling business outcome in one sentence?

If not, reframe before presenting.

## Coaching Frameworks

### Demo Coaching Model
When coaching SEs on executive demos:

| Level | What to Coach | How to Coach |
|---|---|---|
| **Narrative** | Story arc, business framing, opening hook | Watch their demo, rewrite the talk track together |
| **Technique** | Pacing, transitions, handling questions | Live role-play with feedback |
| **Presence** | Confidence, executive register, handling silence | Shadow a DSE-led demo, then debrief |

### The "So What" Drill
After every feature or capability shown in a demo, the SE should be able to answer:
1. **So what?** — Why does this matter to the business?
2. **Compared to what?** — How is this better than their current state?
3. **Says who?** — What proof do we have?

If any answer is weak, the demo beat needs rework.
