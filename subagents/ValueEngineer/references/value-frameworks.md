## ROI Calculation Methodology

### Step 1: Baseline Current-State Costs

Quantify the current cost of the processes that Salesforce will improve:

| Cost Type | How to Calculate | Data Sources |
|---|---|---|
| Labor | FTEs × loaded cost × % time on process | HR data, time studies, manager estimates |
| Technology | License fees + maintenance + infrastructure | IT finance, vendor invoices |
| Process waste | Error rate × rework cost × volume | Quality reports, ticket systems |
| Revenue leakage | Lost deals × avg deal size, or churn × LTV | CRM data, finance |
| Opportunity cost | Value of what people would do instead | Management estimates |

### Step 2: Quantify Benefits

For each benefit, establish:

1. **Baseline metric**: the current measured value
2. **Improvement assumption**: expected % change (cite source)
3. **Financial conversion**: how the metric improvement translates to dollars
4. **Ramp schedule**: when the benefit begins and reaches full run rate
5. **Confidence level**: probability weight for risk-adjusted value

**Financial conversion formulas**:
- **Headcount avoidance**: (Cases deflected × AHT saved) ÷ productive hours per FTE × loaded cost
- **Productivity gain**: Hours saved per person × number of people × loaded hourly rate × value capture rate (typically 50–75%)
- **Revenue uplift**: Pipeline × win rate improvement × average deal size
- **Churn reduction**: Customers retained × annual revenue per customer × gross margin
- **Cost elimination**: Direct cost of system/process being replaced

### Step 3: Net Present Value

```
NPV = Σ (Benefit_t - Cost_t) / (1 + r)^t
```

Where:
- `t` = year (0, 1, 2, 3)
- `r` = discount rate (customer's WACC or 10% default)
- `Benefit_t` = risk-adjusted benefits in year t
- `Cost_t` = all costs in year t

### Step 4: Sensitivity Analysis

Vary the top 3 assumptions independently by ±20%:

1. Identify which assumptions have the largest impact on NPV
2. Calculate break-even thresholds (how wrong can each assumption be before NPV = 0?)
3. Present as a tornado chart for executive audiences
4. Run Monte Carlo simulation if the audience is financially sophisticated

### Discount Rate Selection Guide

| Scenario | Suggested Rate | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Customer provides WACC | Use their WACC | Most accurate for their context |
| Large enterprise, stable industry | 8–10% | Low risk premium |
| Mid-market, moderate growth | 10–12% | Standard risk premium |
| High-growth / volatile industry | 12–15% | Higher risk premium |
| Public sector / nonprofit | 3–5% | Social discount rate |

### Risk Adjustment Weights

| Benefit Type | Confidence Range | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Hard cost elimination (license removal, headcount) | 80–95% | Contractual and measurable |
| Productivity gains | 70–85% | Measurable but depends on adoption |
| Revenue uplift | 50–70% | Multiple factors influence revenue |
| Risk avoidance (compliance, penalties) | 30–60% | Probabilistic by nature |
| Strategic / intangible (brand, employee experience) | Exclude from NPV | Mention qualitatively, don't quantify |

## Value Selling Conversation Framework

### Phase 1: Discovery — Uncover Quantifiable Pain

**Goal**: Leave discovery with 3–5 pain points that have numbers attached.

**Opening frame**: "I'd like to spend some time understanding the business impact of the challenges you're facing — not just what's hard, but what it costs."

**Question patterns**:

| Question Type | Example | What It Yields |
|---|---|---|
| Volume × cost | "How many cases per month? What's your cost per case?" | Direct cost baseline |
| Time × people | "How many hours per week does your team spend on [process]? How many people?" | Productivity baseline |
| Rate × impact | "What's your churn rate? What's the average lifetime value of a customer?" | Revenue leakage |
| Frequency × severity | "How often does [problem] occur? What does it cost to fix each time?" | Process waste |
| Comparison | "Where does your [metric] sit vs. industry average?" | Benchmark gap |
| Aspiration | "If you could fix this, what would your team do with the time back?" | Opportunity cost |

### Phase 2: Validation — Confirm with Customer Data

**Goal**: Replace your estimates with their actual numbers.

- Share preliminary calculations with the economic buyer or champion
- Ask them to validate or correct your assumptions
- Use their numbers in the final model — it becomes "their" business case
- Document where they provided data vs. where you used benchmarks

### Phase 3: Modeling — Build Collaboratively

**Goal**: Co-create the business case so the customer owns the outcome.

- Present methodology first, then populate with their data
- Walk through each assumption transparently
- Let them adjust assumptions — builds ownership
- Run scenarios together (conservative / expected / optimistic)

### Phase 4: Socialization — Arm the Champion

**Goal**: Help your internal champion present the case to their leadership.

- Create a one-page executive summary they can forward
- Prepare them for CFO questions ("where did the 23% come from?")
- Offer to join the internal presentation if appropriate
- Provide a "frequently challenged assumptions" document

### Phase 5: Realization — Prove It

**Goal**: Track actual value delivered vs. business case projections.

- Baseline KPIs before go-live
- Set up value realization dashboard (see deliverable spec)
- Conduct 90-day, 6-month, and 12-month value reviews
- Use results to justify expansion, additional investment, or renewal

## Common Executive Objections & Responses

### "Your numbers seem too high"
- **Response**: "Let me show you exactly how we calculated each figure. Here's the formula, here are the inputs, and here's the benchmark source. Which assumption feels off? Let's adjust it together."
- **Technique**: Offer to use their numbers. Run sensitivity analysis showing NPV is positive even at 50% of projected benefit.

### "We can't measure that"
- **Response**: "That's common at this stage. Here's what we recommend: baseline these three metrics before implementation, then track them quarterly. If we can't measure the primary metric, here are two proxy metrics we can use."
- **Technique**: Propose proxy metrics. Offer to help set up measurement as part of implementation.

### "The implementation cost outweighs the benefit"
- **Response**: "Looking at Year 1 in isolation, the investment is larger. Here's the 3-year view where cumulative benefits are $X.XM vs. $X.XM investment. And here's what doing nothing costs over the same period."
- **Technique**: Show the time-value curve. Pair with cost of inaction analysis.

### "Our CFO won't approve without hard savings"
- **Response**: "Understood. Let me separate the business case into two tiers: hard savings that hit the P&L directly, and productivity gains that free capacity. The hard savings alone deliver $X.XM — a positive ROI on their own."
- **Technique**: Lead with headcount avoidance, license elimination, vendor consolidation. Present productivity as upside.

### "We tried this before and didn't see ROI"
- **Response**: "That's valuable context. What was measured, and when? In our experience, value realization gaps come from three places: adoption, measurement, or scope. This time, we build the measurement plan into the implementation — you'll see the numbers quarterly."
- **Technique**: Diagnose the prior failure. Propose value realization tracking from day one.

### "Can you guarantee these results?"
- **Response**: "We can't guarantee specific outcomes because results depend on adoption and execution. What we can do is show you the methodology, let you stress-test every assumption, and build a tracking system so you see exactly what's being delivered."
- **Technique**: Shift from guarantees to transparency and accountability.

### "Our situation is unique — benchmarks don't apply"
- **Response**: "You're right that every company is different. That's why we've used your actual data for X of the Y inputs. The benchmarks inform the remaining assumptions, and we've used conservative ranges. Let me show you which inputs are yours vs. estimated."
- **Technique**: Highlight how much of the model uses their data. Offer to refine benchmark-based assumptions with a pilot measurement.

## Value Realization Tracking Patterns

### What to Measure Post-Implementation

| Phase | Timing | Focus | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | Pre-go-live (T-30 days) | Capture current-state metrics | Snapshot all KPIs in business case |
| Early signal | Go-live + 30 days | Adoption and activity metrics | User logins, feature usage, data quality |
| Short-term value | Go-live + 90 days | Leading indicators of benefit | Efficiency metrics, process time, deflection |
| Medium-term value | Go-live + 6 months | Benefit categories with financial impact | Cost per case, rep productivity, conversion |
| Full realization | Go-live + 12 months | Business case comparison | Actual vs. projected for all benefit categories |
| Sustained value | Annually thereafter | Ongoing value and expansion case | Year-over-year trends, expansion ROI |

### Value Review Meeting Template

1. **Business case recap** (1 slide): what we projected
2. **Actual results** (2–3 slides): what's been delivered, with variance analysis
3. **Adoption metrics** (1 slide): usage driving (or limiting) value
4. **Lessons learned** (1 slide): what's working, what needs adjustment
5. **Next horizon** (1 slide): expansion opportunities with incremental ROI

### Establishing Credible Baselines

- Measure for at least 30 days before go-live (60 preferred)
- Use the same data source and calculation method pre- and post-implementation
- Document any external factors that affect comparability (seasonality, market shifts, org changes)
- Get stakeholder sign-off on the baseline numbers before implementation begins
